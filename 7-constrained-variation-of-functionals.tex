\section{Constrained Variation of Functionals}

To optimise a functional \(F\bqty{y}\) subject to a functional constraint \(P\bqty{y} = c\), some constant, we can introduce a Lagrange multiplier
\[
    \Phi_\lambda \bqty{y} = F\bqty{y} - \lambda \pqty{P\bqty{y} - c}.
\]

If we optimise with respect to \(y\), we have
\[
    \fdv{F}{y} - \lambda \fdv{P}{y} = 0,
\]
and optimising with respect to \(\lambda\), we have
\[
    P\bqty{y} = c.
\]

\begin{example}
    We determine the \(y\pqty{x}\) that minimises
    \[
        \int_{0}^{1} \frac{1}{2} \pqty{y'}^2 \dd{x}
    \]
    subject to the constraint
    \[
        \int_{0}^{1} y\pqty{x} = 1
    \]
    in the class of smooth functions on \(\ccintv{0}{1}\) with \(y\pqty{0} = y\pqty{1} = 0\). We have
    \begin{align*}
        \Phi_\lambda \bqty{y} &= \int_{0}^{1} \frac{1}{2} y'^2 \dd{x} - \lambda \pqty{\int_{0}^{1} y \dd{x} - 1} \\
        &= \int_{0}^{1} \pqty{\frac{1}{2} y'^2 - \lambda y} \dd{x} + \lambda.
    \end{align*}

    Let \(f = \frac{1}{2} y'^2 - \lambda y\). Euler--Lagrange Equation gives
    \[
        0 = \pdv{f}{y} - \dv{x}\pdv{f}{y'} = -\lambda - y'',
    \]
    and integrating twice gives
    \[
        y = - \frac{1}{2} \lambda x^2 + Ax + B
    \]
    for some constant \(A, B = 0\).

    Boundary conditions on \(y\) gives \(B = 0\) and \(A = \frac{1}{2} \lambda\). The constraint gives us
    \[
        1 = \int_{0}^{1} y \dd{x} = \bqty{- \frac{1}{6} \lambda x^3 + \frac{1}{2} \pqty{\frac{1}{2} \lambda} x^2}_{0}^{1} = \frac{\lambda}{!2}
    \]
    so \(\lambda = 12\), and \(y = 6x \pqty{1 - x}\).
\end{example}

\subsection{Sturm--Liouville Problem}

Consider the class of functions
\[
    \contClass = \set{\functiondomain{y}{\ccintv{\alpha}{\beta}}{\RR}}{y \text{ is }\contClass{2}\text{ and }y\pqty{\alpha} = y\pqty{\beta} = 0}.
\]

Let
\[
    F\bqty{y} = \int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \bqty{\rho y'^2 + \sigma y^2} \dd{x}
\]
and
\[
    G\bqty{y} = \int_{\alpha}^{\beta} w y^2 \dd{X}
\]
where \(\rho, w > 0\) on \(\oointv{\alpha}{\beta}\), and all of \(\rho, \sigma, w\) are functions on \(\ccintv{\alpha}{\beta}\). We want to minimise \(F\bqty{y}\) in \(\contClassSymb\) subject to constraint \(G\bqty{y} = 1\).
